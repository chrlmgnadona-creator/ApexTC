Capital Allocation STRATEGIC SIZING

Optimizing Risk-Adjusted Returns

Capital allocation is the process of deciding how to distribute your financial resources among different trading strategies, asset classes, or individual positions. Proper allocation is the primary driver of portfolio performance over the long term.

Core Allocation Methods

  • Fixed Fractional Sizing: Allocating a constant percentage of your equity to each trade, which naturally scales your position size as your account balance grows or shrinks.
  • Kelly Criterion: A mathematical formula used to determine the optimal size of a series of bets based on your win probability and payoff ratio.
  • Volatility-Based Allocation: Sizing positions based on the asset's current volatility (e.g., Average True Range), ensuring that high-volatility assets occupy a smaller portion of the portfolio to equalize risk across all trades.

The Concentration vs. Diversification Balance

Over-diversification leads to mediocrity, while over-concentration leads to ruin. The ultimate goal of capital allocation is to achieve a concentration high enough to generate significant alpha, while maintaining diversification sufficient to mitigate the risk of a total account wipeout.
