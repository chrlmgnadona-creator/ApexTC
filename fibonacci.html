Fibonacci RETRACEMENT LEVELS

Mathematical Market Structure

Fibonacci retracement levels are horizontal lines that indicate where support or resistance is likely to occur. They are based on the Fibonacci sequence and are used to identify potential trend reversals or continuations in price action.

Key Retracement Levels

  • 23.6%: Often acts as a shallow retracement in very strong trends.
  • 38.2%: A common pullback level in healthy market structures.
  • 50.0%: While not a true Fibonacci number, it is widely respected as a psychological midpoint.
  • 61.8% (The Golden Ratio): The most significant level; a failure to hold this level often signals a trend change.
  • 78.6%: A deep retracement level often seen before a complete trend reversal.
