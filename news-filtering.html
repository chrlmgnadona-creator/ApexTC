News Filtering SIGNAL VS NOISE

Managing Market Information

In the digital age, financial markets are flooded with data. News filtering is the process of stripping away "noise" (irrelevant information) to isolate the "signals" (market-moving events) that require immediate action.

Central Bank Statements HIGH IMPACT
Earnings Reports HIGH IMPACT
General Commentary LOW IMPACT

Filtering Strategies

  • Categorization: Grouping news by asset class, sector, or geographic impact.
  • Source Credibility: Prioritizing primary sources (official government releases) over secondary analysis.
  • Timing Filters: Ignoring historical data in favor of real-time developments that affect current session volatility.

The Importance of Context

A news event's impact is not absolute; it is relative to market expectations. Filtering requires not just knowing the news, but understanding whether the market has already "priced in" the outcome.
