Scalping HIGH FREQUENCY

High-Frequency Execution

Scalping is a trading strategy that attempts to make many small profits on small price changes. It requires a disciplined exit strategy, as one large loss could eliminate many small gains.

Core Principles

  • Tight Spreads: Scalpers rely on assets with high liquidity and low transaction costs.
  • Speed: Execution is paramount; entries and exits often happen in seconds or minutes.
  • Volume-Based: Heavily relies on Order Flow and Level 2 data rather than long-term technical indicators.

Risk Management

Because scalping involves leverage and high frequency, risk per trade must be extremely low. Strict stop-losses are mandatory to prevent slippage from eroding the capital base.
