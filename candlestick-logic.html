Decoding Market Sentiment

Candlesticks are a visual representation of price movement over a specific period. They provide four key pieces of data: the Open, High, Low, and Close (OHLC). The "logic" behind them lies in understanding the battle between buyers and sellers.

The Anatomy of Logic

  • Body Size: Indicates the strength of the move. A large body suggests dominant momentum.
  • Wick Length: Represents rejection. Long wicks show that buyers or sellers were pushed back from price extremes.
  • Color: Green (or white) signifies buying pressure, while red (or black) signifies selling pressure.

Context Matters

A candlestick in isolation is just noise. Candlestick logic only becomes powerful when it is interpreted in the context of the overall trend, support/resistance levels, and volume.
