Wyckoff Theory MARKET CYCLES

The Law of Accumulation and Distribution

Richard Wyckoff’s theory focuses on market cycles and the interaction between supply and demand. It posits that the market is not random, but follows logical cycles driven by large institutional participants ("Composite Man").

Three Fundamental Laws

  • Law of Supply and Demand: Price rises when demand exceeds supply and falls when supply exceeds demand.
  • Law of Cause and Effect: To have an effect, there must be a cause. Accumulation (cause) leads to a Markup (effect).
  • Law of Effort vs. Result: Divergence between volume (effort) and price action (result) often signals a reversal.
