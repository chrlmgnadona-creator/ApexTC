Confluence STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT

Building High-Probability Setups

Confluence occurs when multiple independent technical signals, structural levels, or market indicators align at the same price point and time. It is the cornerstone of high-probability trading, as it reduces the reliance on a single metric.

Elements of Confluence

  • Structural Levels: Supply/Demand zones or major support/resistance levels.
  • Indicator Alignment: RSI divergence, moving average intersections, or specific volume profile clusters.
  • Timeframe Synchronization: When key levels or patterns appear identically across multiple timeframes (e.g., 4H and 15M).
  • Market Context: Fundamental events or session-specific volatility windows that match the technical setup.

The Power of Convergence

The more independent variables that align at a single point, the greater the statistical significance of that area. However, it is essential to balance confluence with simplicity; over-filtering setups by requiring too many conditions can lead to "paralysis by analysis," missing quality trades entirely.
