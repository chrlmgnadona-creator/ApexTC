Backtesting STRATEGY VALIDATION

Historical Performance Analysis

Backtesting is the process of testing a trading strategy on historical data to see how it would have performed. By simulating trades based on past market behavior, you can statistically assess the viability of your edge before risking real capital.

Critical Considerations

  • Data Quality: Garbage in, garbage out. Ensure your historical data is accurate and reflects actual market conditions.
  • Slippage & Commissions: Failure to account for execution costs often leads to overly optimistic results.
  • Overfitting: Avoid optimizing your strategy too perfectly for a specific past period, which may cause it to fail in future market cycles.

Validation Workflow

A robust backtest involves In-Sample testing (the data used to build the rules) and Out-of-Sample testing (testing on data the strategy has never "seen" before) to ensure statistical significance.
