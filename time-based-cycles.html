Time-Based Cycles TEMPORAL ANALYSIS

Understanding Market Seasonality

Markets do not move randomly; they often exhibit repetitive tendencies based on time. Time-based cycles involve identifying recurring patterns that occur over specific intervals, such as daily, weekly, monthly, or even yearly periods, driven by institutional behaviors and macroeconomic reporting schedules.

Common Temporal Cycles

  • Intraday Cycles: Patterns that repeat during specific sessions, such as the "London Open" or "New York Lunch" volatility dips.
  • Weekly Cycles: The tendency for trends to initiate at the start of the week and consolidate toward the Friday close.
  • Quarterly Rebalancing: Massive institutional volume inflows and outflows occurring at the end of each fiscal quarter to rebalance portfolios.
  • Seasonality: Historical tendencies for specific assets to perform better during certain months (e.g., "Sell in May and go away").

The Importance of Time Context

Successful timing involves matching your trade setups to the relevant temporal cycle. A breakout signal that appears during a low-volatility session window often lacks the "fuel" to sustain a move, whereas the same signal appearing during a high-liquidity temporal cycle is significantly more likely to succeed.
