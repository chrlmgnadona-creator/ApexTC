Swing Failures REVERSAL PATTERNS

Detecting Exhaustion

A Swing Failure Pattern (SFP) occurs when price attempts to break above a previous swing high (or below a previous swing low) but fails to maintain that level, quickly reversing back into the previous range. This is a primary indicator of liquidity trapping and trend exhaustion.

Core Mechanics

  • The Trap: Retail traders often place stop-losses just beyond clear swing highs/lows. Large institutions "sweep" these levels to fill their orders.
  • The Rejection: If price cannot find follow-through momentum after sweeping these liquidity zones, the reversal is often swift and sharp.
  • Confirmation: An SFP is generally confirmed by a candlestick close back inside the previous swing range, often accompanied by increased volume.

Strategic Application

SFPs are high-probability setups when they align with higher-timeframe areas of interest (e.g., support/resistance or supply/demand zones). By focusing on where "everyone else" is likely to have their stop-losses, you can identify the exact points where smart money is likely to create a reversal.
