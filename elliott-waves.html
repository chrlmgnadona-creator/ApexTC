Market Psychology in Cycles

Elliott Wave Theory posits that financial markets move in repetitive cycles determined by collective investor psychology. It identifies a specific 5-wave trend pattern followed by a 3-wave corrective pattern.

The 5-3 Sequence

  • Impulse Waves (1, 3, 5): These waves move in the direction of the main trend.
  • Corrective Waves (2, 4): These represent temporary retracements against the main trend.
  • ABC Correction: A 3-wave structure that typically follows the completion of the 5-wave impulse sequence.

Core Rules

  • Wave 2 can never retrace more than 100% of Wave 1.
  • Wave 3 is often the longest and never the shortest of the impulse waves.
  • Wave 4 cannot overlap with the price territory of Wave 1.
