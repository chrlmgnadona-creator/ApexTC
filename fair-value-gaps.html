Fair Value Gaps FVG

Visualizing the Inefficiency

The highlighted zone shows the price gap where the 1st candle's high and 3rd candle's low do not overlap. The gold line represents price returning to mitigate this gap.

Types of FVGs

Bullish FVG Bearish FVG
Forms in an upward move where the candle bodies do not overlap. Forms in a downward move where the candle bodies do not overlap.

Why do they matter?

  • Magnetic Effect: Price often returns to "fill" or "mitigate" the gap before continuing its original trend.
  • Momentum Indicator: A large FVG indicates high conviction and strong institutional interest.
  • Confluence: Traders often look for FVGs that overlap with Order Blocks to increase the probability of a successful trade entry.
