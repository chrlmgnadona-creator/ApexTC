Hedging RISK MITIGATION

Protecting Against Adverse Moves

Hedging is a risk management strategy employed to offset losses in investments by taking an opposite position in a related asset. The goal is to reduce volatility and protect your portfolio from unfavorable price movements.

Core Strategies

  • Options Hedging: Using puts to protect against a decline in the value of an existing long equity position.
  • Diversification: Allocating capital across non-correlated assets to ensure that a decline in one sector is offset by stability in another.
  • Futures Contracts: Locking in a price for an asset today to mitigate the risk of price fluctuation in the future.

The Cost-Benefit Tradeoff

Hedging is rarely "free." While it provides protection, it often limits potential upside. The key to successful hedging is not eliminating all risk, but managing it to a level where the portfolio remains resilient to extreme market events.
