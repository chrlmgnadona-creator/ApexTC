Understanding Liquidity Sweeps

A Liquidity Sweep occurs when price briefly pushes past a known support or resistance level to trigger retail stop-loss orders before reversing.

Visualizing the Stop-Run

The red candle represents the "sweep" candle that breaks the support line (dashed) to gather liquidity before the market snaps back.

How to Identify a Sweep

Phase Description
Consolidation Retail traders place stops above/below support levels.
The Sweep A quick, sharp spike breaks the level, hitting those stops.
Reversal Price fails to hold the break and moves aggressively in the original direction.
