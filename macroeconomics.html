Macroeconomics examines the economy as a whole, focusing on phenomena such as inflation, price levels, rate of economic growth, national income, gross domestic product (GDP), and changes in unemployment.

Primary Objectives

  • Price Stability: Controlling inflation to maintain purchasing power.
  • Full Employment: Minimizing cyclical unemployment within the labor force.
  • Economic Growth: Sustaining increases in the production of goods and services over time.

Policy Tools

Governments and central banks manage the macro-economy primarily through Fiscal Policy (taxation and spending) and Monetary Policy (interest rates and money supply).
