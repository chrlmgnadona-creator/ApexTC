The Philosophy of Price Action

Price action is the practice of making trading decisions based on the movement of a security's price over time. Unlike lagging indicators, price action focuses on the raw data of the market, allowing traders to observe the real-time battle between buyers and sellers.

Developing a Strategy

  1. Context: Align with the higher timeframe trend.
  2. Confluence: Use multiple factors to confirm setup.
  3. Risk Management: Maintain a 1:2 risk-to-reward ratio.

Market Structure

Understanding the "flow" of the market is paramount to price action.

  • Higher Highs (HH) & Higher Lows (HL): Indicates a Bullish Trend.
  • Lower Highs (LH) & Lower Lows (LL): Indicates a Bearish Trend.
  • Range/Sideways: Price is oscillating between two defined horizontal levels.

Support & Resistance (S&R)

These are "zones of interest" where market participants historically make decisions.

Support

A price level where demand is strong enough to stop the price from falling further.

Resistance

A price level where supply is strong enough to prevent the price from rising higher.
