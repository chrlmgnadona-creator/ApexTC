Forward Testing REAL-TIME VALIDATION

From Simulation to Real-Time

Forward testing, often referred to as "paper trading," involves testing a trading strategy in real-time market conditions without using actual capital. This step bridges the gap between historical backtesting and live market execution.

Active Simulation Mode

Validating strategy logic against current live price feeds to detect slippage, execution delays, and real-time edge consistency.

Why Forward Testing is Essential

  • Execution Realism: Tests how your platform handles order routing, latency, and real-world liquidity.
  • Psychological Testing: Allows you to practice discipline and emotional regulation without the stress of financial loss.
  • Market Nuance: Identifies if a strategy that worked historically holds up in current market sentiment and volatility regimes.

The Bridge to Live Trading

Forward testing is the final gatekeeper before live deployment. A successful forward test should typically cover at least one full market cycle to ensure the strategy is not only profitable but also resilient to various market states.
