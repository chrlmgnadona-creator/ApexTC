Gap Trading PRICE ANOMALIES

Understanding Price Gaps

A price gap is an empty space on a chart where no trading has occurred, typically occurring between the close of one session and the open of the next due to news, earnings, or major economic events.

Types of Gaps

  • Breakaway Gap: Signals the start of a new trend, occurring after a period of consolidation.
  • Runaway Gap (Measuring Gap): Occurs during a strong trend, indicating high conviction and momentum.
  • Exhaustion Gap: Appears at the end of a trend, signaling that the move has run out of steam and a reversal may follow.

The "Gap Fill" Theory

Many traders follow the "gap fill" hypothesis, which suggests that markets have a tendency to move back to the price level where the gap originated, effectively "filling" the empty space as sentiment normalizes.
