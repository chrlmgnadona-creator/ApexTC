Leverage Math RISK AMPLIFICATION

The Mechanics of Margin

Leverage allows traders to control large positions with a relatively small amount of capital. While this amplifies potential returns, it mathematically accelerates the proximity of your liquidation point.

$$LiquidationPrice = EntryPrice \times \left(1 \pm \frac{1}{Leverage}\right)$$
$$ROI_{Leveraged} = ROI_{Asset} \times Leverage$$

The Asymmetry of Loss

It is vital to understand that a loss is not recouped linearly when using leverage. As leverage increases, the percentage gain required to recover from a percentage loss grows exponentially.

  • 2x Leverage: A 25% drop results in a 50% loss.
  • 10x Leverage: A 10% drop results in a 100% loss (Total Liquidation).
  • The "Volatility Tax": High leverage makes positions extremely sensitive to minor market noise, often triggering stop-losses or liquidations before a trend can play out.

Risk Awareness

Always calculate your Distance to Liquidation before entering a trade. If the percentage move required to trigger a liquidation is within the normal daily volatility range of the asset, the position is mathematically over-leveraged regardless of the trade thesis.
