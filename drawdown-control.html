Managing Peak-to-Trough Decline

A drawdown is the peak-to-trough decline during a specific period for an investment or trading account. Managing drawdown is not just about profitability; it is about ensuring account longevity and preventing the "mathematical impossibility" of recovery from massive losses.

Core Principles

  • Hard Stop-Losses: Predetermined levels to exit positions before a minor loss turns into a catastrophic drawdown.
  • Volatility Sizing: Reducing position sizes during high-volatility regimes to keep exposure within acceptable risk limits.
  • Max Drawdown Limits: Implementing an "equity circuit breaker"—stepping away from the market entirely if a specified percentage of total account capital is lost.

The Recovery Math

Recovery math is unforgiving. A 10% drawdown requires an 11% return to break even, but a 50% drawdown requires a 100% return to return to the original starting capital. Controlling the depth of your drawdowns is the most important factor in long-term compounding.
