Execution Speed LATENCY DYNAMICS

The Impact of Latency on Fill Quality

Execution speed refers to the time elapsed between an order being sent from your platform and the moment it is filled by the exchange. In fast-moving markets, even millisecond delays can lead to "slippage," where your order is filled at a worse price than expected.

Primary Latency Factors

  • Network Latency: The physical distance and number of hops between your server and the exchange’s matching engine.
  • Processing Latency: The time required for the exchange to receive, validate, and match your order against existing liquidity.
  • Order Type Impact: Market orders are generally faster but prone to slippage, while limit orders provide price certainty but risk non-execution during high volatility.

Optimizing for Throughput

For algorithmic and high-frequency strategies, reducing execution latency is a competitive necessity. This is often achieved through co-location of trading servers with exchange data centers, the use of low-latency communication protocols, and optimizing code to minimize the CPU time required to calculate and dispatch orders.
