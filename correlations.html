Asset Correlations MARKET RELATIONSHIPS

Inter-market Dependencies

Correlation measures the degree to which two assets move in relation to one another. Understanding these relationships is vital for portfolio diversification and hedging risks.

Key Types

  • Positive Correlation: Assets tend to move in the same direction (e.g., Oil and Energy stocks).
  • Negative Correlation: Assets move in opposite directions (e.g., Gold and the US Dollar).
  • Uncorrelated: Asset price movements show no statistical relationship, often used for effective diversification.

Strategic Application

Correlations are not static; they change based on market regimes. During periods of extreme volatility or liquidity crises, correlations often converge to 1.0, rendering traditional diversification less effective precisely when it is needed most.
