Arbitrage PRICE INEFFICIENCIES

Exploiting Market Discrepancies

Arbitrage is the practice of taking advantage of a price difference between two or more markets. It involves the simultaneous purchase and sale of an asset in different markets to profit from a tiny difference in the asset's listed price.

The Formula:
$$Profit = (Price_{High} - Price_{Low}) \times Quantity - TransactionCosts$$

Common Arbitrage Types

  • Spatial Arbitrage: Buying an asset in one geographic location and selling it in another.
  • Triangular Arbitrage: Exploiting exchange rate discrepancies between three different currencies.
  • Statistical Arbitrage: Using complex mathematical models to identify temporary price deviations between correlated assets.

The Role in Market Efficiency

Arbitrageurs are essential to market health. By constantly seeking out and closing price gaps, they ensure that assets are priced consistently across different platforms, ultimately driving markets toward a state of equilibrium.
