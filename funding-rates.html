Funding Rates PERPETUAL SWAPS

Market Sentiment Equilibrium

Funding rates are periodic payments either to traders that are long or short based on the difference between perpetual contract markets and spot prices. They ensure the derivative price stays anchored to the underlying asset.

Key Mechanics

  • Positive Rate: Longs pay shorts. Indicates market is bullish; leverage is skewed to the upside.
  • Negative Rate: Shorts pay longs. Indicates market is bearish; leverage is skewed to the downside.
  • Mean Reversion: Extremely high or low rates often signal a potential local top or bottom due to excessive leverage.

Trading Implications

Savvy traders track funding rates to identify "crowded trades." When funding reaches extreme levels, it often precedes a violent "long squeeze" or "short squeeze" as the market forces over-leveraged participants to close positions.
