Moving Averages TREND SMOOTHING

Smoothing Market Noise

A Moving Average (MA) is a technical indicator that calculates the average price of an asset over a specific number of time periods, smoothing out short-term fluctuations to reveal the underlying trend.

$$SMA = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} P_i}{n}$$
Where $P$ is the price at period $i$ and $n$ is the total number of periods.

Types of Averages

  • Simple Moving Average (SMA): Gives equal weight to all data points in the period.
  • Exponential Moving Average (EMA): Places greater weight on recent prices, making it more responsive to new information.

Trend Identification

Moving averages act as dynamic support and resistance levels. When price is above the moving average, the trend is generally considered bullish; when price is below, the trend is bearish. Traders often look for "Golden Crosses" or "Death Crosses" as signals of major trend shifts.
