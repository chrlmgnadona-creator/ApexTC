Stop-Loss Logic RISK PROTECTION

Defining Tactical Exit Points

Stop-loss logic is the algorithmic or discretionary framework used to protect capital by automatically exiting a position when the market moves against the trade thesis. It is the definitive line between a manageable loss and a catastrophic account drawdown.

Primary Stop-Loss Methodologies

  • Structural Stops: Placed beyond key market structure points (e.g., above recent highs or below recent lows). These are effective because the trade thesis is invalidated if these levels are breached.
  • Volatility-Based Stops: Utilizing indicators like Average True Range (ATR) to set stops that expand or contract based on current market noise, preventing premature "stop-outs" during high volatility.
  • Time-Based Stops: Exiting a trade simply because it has failed to reach its target within an expected timeframe, preventing capital from being tied up in stagnant positions.
  • Percentage-Based Stops: A fixed percentage of equity risk per trade, ensuring that no single loss can disproportionately impact the total portfolio balance.

Protecting the Equity Curve

The goal of stop-loss logic is not to be "right" on every trade, but to ensure that when you are wrong, the cost of being wrong is small and controlled. A robust stop-loss strategy allows you to survive a string of losses, keeping you in the game until a high-probability setup finally aligns in your favor.
