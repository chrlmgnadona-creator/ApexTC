Volatility MARKET AMPLITUDE

The Pulse of the Market

Volatility measures the degree of variation in an asset's price over time. It is essentially the "speedometer" of the market—high volatility indicates large, rapid price swings, while low volatility suggests a calm, range-bound market.

Current Market Regime: Moderate

Scale: Low Volatility (Green) to High Volatility (Red)

Key Concepts

  • Historical Volatility (HV): Based on past price movements; it tells us how volatile an asset has been.
  • Implied Volatility (IV): Derived from option prices; it represents the market's expectation of future volatility.
  • Volatility Clustering: The tendency for periods of high volatility to be followed by more high volatility.

Trading Volatility

Volatility is often mistaken for risk, but for traders, it represents opportunity. Strategies such as options straddles, breakout trading, and mean reversion are directly tied to the expansion and contraction of market volatility.
